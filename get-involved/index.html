Launch

Launch is a quarter-long student-led incubator program where you will learn everything from ideation to execution and turn napkin sketches into real, scalable businesses. Learn the ins and outs of an entrepreneurial mindset, then embark on an entrepreneurial journey (often for the first time) through an action-driven curriculum. Participants will form teams to start new companies that they build throughout the quarter, culminating in a Demo Day. In the last 2 years we’ve helped 100+ students start over 25 new companies; you could be next.

Teams

Branding

Many local companies and start-ups at Northwestern/Evanston have great products made by talented teams. However, sometimes marketing those products can be difficult without the adequate help or marketing strategies. Thus, Branding comes into play in which we help enable those companies to accelerate their businesses based on their targeted audiences. As Branding, we will focus our work on two parts: one being the internal work of EPIC and the other focusing on external groups such as local businesses and start-ups. We want to utilize our member’s skill sets to make a definite impact on these businesses as if it was a real-world profession.

Operations

As one of the most important parts in EPIC’s internal affairs, Operations team strives to create a helpful, harmonious and friendly environment for EPIC. By organizing various social events and planning out workshops, we connects different departments together by giving them an opportunity to communicate and learn from each other. Operations dedicates itself to help EPIC grow as a complete whole.

Tech

Technology is crucial for the implementation of entrepreneurial endeavors. Tech aims to bring exposures and experiences to students through technical projects in the Northwestern ecosystem. Tech started out as an IT department for EPIC. We built EPIC’s first website and hosted servers for other teams within EPIC. Two years ago, we decided to invest in our own ideas and grow our members’ technical and entrepreneurial skills through personal projects. The projects were used as a training system for our members to learn new languages and tools. Last year, with those new skills, Tech took on the task of designing and implementing an application for Brew Bike. Currently, the members are on a new trajectory to work on Northwestern-focused projects that provide value for the Northwestern community.

Women in Tech

Women in Tech equips women on campus with the necessary skills and networks to become successful leaders in the world of tech, innovation, and entrepreneurship. We aim to leverage Northwestern’s connections to startups and prominent tech companies in order to provide career resources and opportunities for female students.
